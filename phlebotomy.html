Looking for A Phlebotomy Medical Training?

Lead Instructor: Dr. Melesse Semegne



    • Fenote Training Academy is a licensed and accredited organization. We offer an phlebotomy course for those who need to be working as a phlebotomist and also our course is designed to prepare students for the National Phlebotomy Exam. Our program combines online and in-person instruction, providing a comprehensive learning experience. 1. What is Phlebotomy? Phlebotomy is the practice of drawing blood from patients for medical testing, transfusions, research, or donations. It's a crucial procedure used in diagnosing and monitoring a patient's health condition. 2. Why Phlebotomy Training? Phlebotomy training is essential because it equips individuals with the skills and knowledge needed to safely and efficiently collect blood samples. The training covers various techniques, patient interaction, safety protocols, and legal aspects of the profession. Phlebotomy is a specialized skill that requires precision, care, and an understanding of human anatomy. 3. Function of Phlebotomy: The primary function of phlebotomy is to obtain blood specimens from patients for laboratory analysis. These samples help in diagnosing diseases, monitoring conditions like diabetes, or determining the levels of various substances in the blood (e.g., cholesterol, glucose). Phlebotomy also plays a role in blood donation, where the collected blood is used for transfusions. 4. The Role of Phlebotomy: Phlebotomists are healthcare professionals who perform the task of drawing blood. Their role involves: Preparing patients for blood collection Performing venipuncture (the process of accessing a vein to draw blood) Ensuring the correct labeling of samples Maintaining patient comfort and safety Following infection control protocols Working closely with laboratory personnel to ensure timely processing of samples

    Online Training

    The online portion is accessible 24/7. This format allows students flexible, on-demand access to course materials. You must complete the online portion before the in-person training portion. Completing your online course will take three weeks. After finishing the online portion at your own pace, you'll attend a in-person training during the fourth week. In our Phlebotomy Online Course topics we cover are: Chapter 1: Introduction to Phlebotomy Definition of Phlebotomy Duties of a Phlebotomist Importance of Phlebotomy in Healthcare Chapter 2: Anatomy and Physiology for Phlebotomists Overview of the Circulatory System Veins and Arteries Commonly Used in Phlebotomy Understanding Blood Composition Chapter 3: Basics Of Phlebotomy Patient Assessment Pre-Analytical Variables and Basic Laboratory Testing Chapter 4 : Phlebotomy Equipment Types of Needles and Syringes Blood Collection Tubes and Their Uses Hazardous Materials Management Chapter 5: Venipuncture and Dermal Puncture Venipuncture Techniques Capillary Puncture Techniques Blood Collection from Pediatric and Geriatric Patients Troubleshooting Difficult Blood Draws Chapter 6: Specimen Handling and Processing Labeling and Transporting Blood Samples Specimens Understanding Laboratory Quality Control in Phlebotomy Chapter: 7 Point of care testing ( POCT ) Chapter: 8 Record Keeping and documentation Chapter 9: Patient Care and Complications Preparing Patients for Blood Collection Addressing Patient Fears and Anxieties Therapeutic communications Post- Venipuncture care and patient instruction Chapter 10: Review and Certification Preparation Phlebotomy Review Questions Practice Scenarios and Case Studies Certification Exam Resources

    In-Person Training

    The in-person training spans 2-3 days and is onsight. These sessions feature engaging, interactive lessons designed to maximize student interaction and provide continuous feedback and support. This hands-on approach allow students to apply their online learning in a practical setting, enhancing their skills and confidence. In our In-person phlebotomy training we cover the following key areas: Venipuncture Techniques: Hands-on practice in drawing blood from veins using various methods. Patient Interaction: Techniques for communicating with patients, ensuring their comfort, and gaining consent. Safety Protocols: Training on infection control, proper use of personal protective equipment (PPE), and safe disposal of needles. Equipment Handling: Familiarization with phlebotomy tools like needles, vacutainers, and tourniquets. Blood Sample Management: Proper labeling, handling, and storage of blood samples to prevent contamination. Emergency Procedures: Responding to complications, such as fainting or adverse reactions during blood draws. Quality Control: Ensuring accurate and reliable results through proper technique and adherence to guidelines. Legal and Ethical Considerations: Understanding patient rights, confidentiality, and regulatory compliance.


    Exam

    We also offer an online exam to further enhance students' knowledge before the National Phlebotomy Exam. This preparatory exam allows students to test their understanding of the course material and identify any areas needing additional study. By simulating the conditions of the actual exam, it helps build confidence and ensures that students are well-prepared for success.
