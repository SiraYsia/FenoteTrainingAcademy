Community Health Worker Training Program

A Community Health Worker (CHW) is a frontline public health professional and a trusted member of the community. They build strong, supportive relationships with individuals and serve as a vital link between patients and the health and social services available to them. By focusing on each person's unique needs beyond the hospital or clinic, CHWs play a key role in helping patients achieve their health goals and improve overall well-being.

How Can a CHW Help?

In collaboration with primary care providers, our Community Health Worker (CHW) supports patients in reaching their health goals and navigating their care plans. A CHW offers educational resources, shares helpful tools such as support groups or wellness apps, and connects patients to additional programs when needed. Acting as an accountability partner, the CHW helps individuals develop the skills to manage chronic conditions, mental health challenges, substance use, addiction, and other specific health concerns. Additionally, our CHW assists patients in accessing both health-related and social services for which they may be eligible.

Population a CHW Serves

  • SD Medicaid patients
  • Individuals with barriers to health or social service supports
  • Individuals struggling with chronic disease self-management

Health Services a CHW Offers

  • Health promotion and coaching
  • Lifestyle and social assistance
  • Financial aid and assistance programs
  • Health system navigation
  • Referrals to healthcare professionals
  • Health Education
  • Prescription medication affordability assistance

Social Services a CHW Can Help Navigate

  • Housing
  • Home assistive devices
  • Food
  • State benefits
  • Daycare
  • Employment
  • Transportation
