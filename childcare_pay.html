CORE OF KNOWLEDGE ASSESSMENT

As required by the Maryland State Department of Education-Office of Child Care (OCC), an assessment is given at the end of all Core of Knowledge classes. This may be in the form of an activity, group project, homework, or written test. To receive a Core of Knowledge certificate, participants must earn a score of 75% or higher. A score of 74% or less on the assessment will result in no certificate. Students may schedule an appointment to retake the test. If the student is still unable to pass the second assessment, the student will need to retake the class (payment required). Core of Knowledge certificates count towards both licens- ing and Maryland child care credential training requirements set forth by OCC. Participants must remain in class until it is completed. If you leave early and miss the assessment, we cannot issue a Core of Knowledge certificate. Also, please note multi-part classes require attendance at all sessions to qualify for the assessment.

CANCELATIONS/REFUNDS/CREDITS

There are no refunds for missed classes or for late arrivals. Cancelations via email or phone (leave a message, if necessary) must be made no less than 24 hours before the training. All classes start and end on time. If you arrive after the registrar has collected the attendance sheet, it will be considered a missed class.

In the event a student wishes to cancel a class, a refund or credit for a future class may be issued. Cancelations must be received at least 24 hours before a class is scheduled to qualify for a refund or credit. A $10 cancelation fee will be deducted if a refund is requested. If the student requests that a credit be issued for a future class, the cancelation fee does not apply. Missed classes will not be refunded or credited (including late arrivals or leaving early).

DUPLICATE CERTIFICATES

All certificates are emailed within 10 business days of class completion. If you misplace a certificate, there is a $10 administrative fee for issuing a duplicate certificate.

CLASS CANCELATIONS

Class schedules are planned months in advance, however there are times when classes must be canceled. Classes are canceled if Montgomery County Public Schools are closed due to inclement weather or if evening/weekend activities are canceled due to inclement weather or another emergency. Classes may also be canceled due to low enrollment or for other administrative reasons. You will be notified by email if a class is canceled. You may also call 240-777-4769 to determine the status of a class. In the event, we cancel a class every effort will be made to reschedule or substitute the class. If the class cannot be resched- uled, a substitution cannot be arranged, or if you are unable to attend the rescheduled class, you will receive a full refund.
