Course Description

This American Red Cross BLS Instructor Course is designed for individuals aiming to become certified BLS instructors. It combines online and in-person training components to deliver a comprehensive learning experience.


Course Requirements

Prerequisites:

Current certification in Basic Life Support (BLS) or equivalent.

Completion of the online course (approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes) prior to attending the in-person session.

Online course access will be provided upon full registration and payment.


Registration Status:

Registrants will remain in pending status until proof of current BLS certification is verified.


Intended Audience

This course is tailored for:


  • Medical Personnel: Physicians, nurses, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, dentists, pharmacists, physical therapists, nurse assistants, and students pursuing these professions.
  • Public Safety Personnel and Responders: Paramedics, advanced EMTs, EMTs, firefighters, law enforcement officers, emergency medical responders (EMRs), and students preparing for these roles.

Course Purpose

The BLS Instructor Course equips participants with the skills and knowledge to recognize and respond to life-threatening emergencies, including:


  • Respiratory arrest or failure
  • Cardiac arrest
  • Airway obstruction
  • Opioid overdose
  • Other critical injuries or illnesses

Emphasis is placed on delivering high-quality care, integrating critical thinking and psychomotor skills, and ensuring optimal patient outcomes.
