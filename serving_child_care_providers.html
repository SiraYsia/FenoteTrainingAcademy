Training Modules

Child Development (CDV)

4 Hours

Essential knowledge and strategies to support children's overall development through understanding milestones, developmental theories, and the role of play.

Curriculum (CUR)

4 Hours

Designing developmentally appropriate curricula and maintaining safe environments that support children's well-being and learning.

Health, Safety & Nutrition (HSN)

4 Hours

Creating a safe and healthy childcare environment, recognizing signs of illness, emergency preparedness, and effective meal planning.

Special Needs (SPN)

4 Hours

Insights into typical and atypical developmental patterns, ADA compliance, and inclusive practices to support all children.

Professionalism (PRO)

4 Hours

Childcare business essentials, MSDE compliance, contract and policy development, and basic business practices.

Community (COM)

4 Hours

Building family partnerships and community ties, cultural influences, and strategies to create a supportive early childhood environment.

❤️ CPR training included with this program

Assessment Requirements

All Core of Knowledge classes require a 75% passing score on final assessments (activities, projects, or tests). Certificates fulfill Maryland childcare credential training requirements. Click "Register Now" to enroll in the 6 Core Knowledge Training Package (CDV, CUR, HSN, SPN, PRO, COM).

Pre-Service Trainings

Essential foundational courses for new childcare providers looking to establish a strong knowledge base in childcare principles

45 Hours Child Growth and Development

45 Hours

This training provides an overview of key stages in child growth and development. Participants will learn about physical, cognitive, social, and emotional milestones, as well as factors influencing development. The course equips childcare providers with strategies to support children's growth and create nurturing learning environments.

45 Hours Preschool Curriculum

45 Hours

This training explores the fundamentals of designing and implementing an effective preschool curriculum. Participants will learn to create engaging, developmentally appropriate activities that support early learning in literacy, math, science, and social-emotional development. The course also covers best practices for lesson planning, assessment, and fostering a positive learning environment.

45 Hours Infant-Toddler Care and Development

45 Hours

Additional Trainings

Specialized courses to enhance specific skills and knowledge areas for childcare professionals

SIDS, SUID and Other Sleep-Related Infant Deaths

2 Hours

This training helps childcare providers understand SIDS, SUID, and other sleep-related infant deaths. Participants will learn to differentiate SIDS and SUID, identify risk factors, create a safe sleep environment, and apply the ABCs of safe sleep. The course provides essential strategies to reduce risks and ensure infant safety.

9 Hours Communication

9 Hours

This training equips childcare providers with essential communication skills for early care and education settings. Participants will develop effective speaking and listening skills, enhance reading comprehension, and improve writing for documentation. The course also covers tools for recording and sharing critical child information to support clear and professional communication.
