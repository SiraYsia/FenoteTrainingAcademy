Your Career Path

Responsibilities

  • Processing and analyzing clinical specimens
  • Performing routine and specialized tests
  • Preparing and staining slides
  • Conducting phlebotomy and recording test results
  • Maintaining lab cleanliness and managing supplies

Work Environment and Salary

You'll work alongside healthcare professionals in various settings such as hospitals, clinics, and labs. Depending on your workplace, you may need to work nights, weekends, and holidays.

The average annual salary for medical lab assistants is approximately $40,593.*

Industry Demand

With the rapid expansion of the healthcare industry, the demand for medical lab assistants is high. The Department of Labor predicts over 1.8 million new healthcare jobs by 2031, making this a promising career path.
